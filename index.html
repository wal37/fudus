What it really is

Not another food tracker. A nutrition intelligence system.

The platform connects food recognition, portion analysis, localized food data, health scoring, and personalized diet intelligence into one product layer for users, clinics, developers, and wellness organizations.

Problem

Most nutrition products still fail on real local meals.

Western datasets dominate

Generic nutrition systems rely on food databases that do not understand local dishes, recipes, or ingredient combinations.

Mixed dishes are hard

Meals like pasta with meatballs, burrito bowls, or roast chicken plates are difficult for basic food tracking tools to decompose correctly.

Portions are inconsistent

Meal sizes vary by serving style, container, and preparation method, which breaks macro and calorie accuracy.

Health guidance is weak

Most apps stop at logging. They do not translate meals into useful warnings, glycemic estimates, or risk-aware recommendations.

Product surface

Built to feel like a health intelligence layer, not a food diary.

The product experience is designed for fast analysis, clear meal feedback, and structured recommendations that can serve consumers, clinics, and enterprise partners.
